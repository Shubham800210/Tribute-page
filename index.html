Mahendra Singh Dhoni

7 July 1981

A Trailblazing Cricket Icon and Inspirational Leader

Mahendra Singh Dhoni born 7 July 1981 is an Indian professional cricketer. He was captain of the Indian national team in limited-overs formats from 2007 to 2017 and in Test cricket from 2008 to 2014. Dhoni is widely considered one of the greatest cricket captains, wicket-keeper-batsman and finishers in the history of cricket.[a] He plays as a right-handed wicket-keeper-batsman and is known for his calm captaincy and his ability to finish matches in tight situations. He is also the current captain of Chennai Super Kings in the Indian Premier League.

Achievements

  • Captaincy Success: Dhoni led the Indian cricket team from 2007 to 2016, becoming one of the most successful captains in the history of the sport. Under his leadership, India achieved significant milestones, including reaching the top of the ICC Test rankings and winning multiple ICC tournaments.
  • ICC World T20 2007: Dhoni led India to victory in the inaugural ICC World Twenty20 held in South Africa. He played crucial innings throughout the tournament and hit the winning runs in the final against Pakistan.
  • ICC Cricket World Cup 2011: Dhoni's most significant achievement came in the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup held in India. He led the team to their first World Cup victory in 28 years, scoring an unbeaten 91 in the final against Sri Lanka and famously sealing the win with a six.
  • Champions Trophy 2013: Dhoni guided India to glory in the ICC Champions Trophy held in England. His tactical acumen and composure were on display as India clinched the title by defeating England in a rain-affected final.
  • No. 1 Test Ranking: Under Dhoni's captaincy, India reached the top of the ICC Test rankings for the first time in 2009 and held the position for multiple years. His leadership played a vital role in shaping a formidable Indian Test team.
  • Numerous Records: Dhoni holds several individual records, including the most international matches as captain for India, the most dismissals by an Indian wicket-keeper, and the highest score by a wicket-keeper in ODIs (183*).
